Co-browsing
Co-browsing (short for collaborative browsing) is the practice of navigating the World Wide Web together in real-time. Co-browsing allows multiple users to interact with the same web page simultaneously while each retaining control of their own cursor.

Originally developed for customer support, co-browsing has expanded into social use cases including watch parties, collaborative shopping, and shared research sessions.
